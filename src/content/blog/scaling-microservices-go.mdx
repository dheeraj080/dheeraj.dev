As systems grow, monolithic architectures often become bottlenecks. Transitioning to microservices can solve scaling issues, but introduces new challenges in inter-service communication.

In this post, we explore why Go is an excellent choice for microservices. Its lightweight goroutines and fast compilation make it ideal for building small, efficient services.

## Why gRPC?

We'll also look at gRPC, a high-performance RPC framework. Unlike REST, gRPC uses Protocol Buffers for binary serialization, resulting in smaller payloads and faster parsing.

By combining Go and gRPC, we can build systems that handle thousands of concurrent requests with minimal latency. We'll cover connection pooling, load balancing, and error handling strategies.

### Observability

Finally, we'll discuss observability—how to trace requests across multiple services using OpenTelemetry to ensure your distributed system remains debuggable.
